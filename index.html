Baseline or Warning Light?

Remixing the Wall Street Journal's February 2026 inflation story by shifting from one monthly snapshot to coordinated views of category pressure, trend persistence, and oil sensitivity.

By Clay Parr Published March 11, 2026

The original Wall Street Journal article argued that February 2026 inflation was calm on paper (2.4% headline, 2.5% core) but potentially fragile because a sudden geopolitical shock could push energy prices higher. This remix keeps that core insight, then asks a different question: how much of inflation risk is really oil, and how much is still embedded in services?

Remix Takeaway

This view-based remix supports the WSJ warning, but with a narrower claim: oil shocks can quickly reheat headline inflation, yet category data suggest that persistent services costs remain a second, slower force. Readers can now test that claim month by month instead of accepting one static frame.
